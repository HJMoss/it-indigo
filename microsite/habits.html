Understanding Behavior

Hedgehogs are solitary, nocturnal animals with a unique set of instincts. Understanding their natural habits is the key to building a bond with your pet.

While they are often shy at first, hedgehogs are curious explorers. They rely heavily on their sense of smell and hearing rather than their eyesight to navigate their surroundings.

Quick Habits

  • Activity: Nocturnal (Night)
  • Social: Solitary
  • Defense: Curling into a ball
  • Navigation: Scent-based

Natural Instincts

Self-Anointing

When a hedgehog encounters a new or interesting scent, they will foam at the mouth and spread the saliva over their quills. It looks strange, but it's completely normal!

Burrowing

In the wild, hedgehogs sleep in burrows or under brush. Pet hedgehogs love fleece blankets, tunnels, or "igloos" where they can feel hidden and secure.

The Night Shift

Hedgehogs are most active between 9:00 PM and 6:00 AM. They can run several miles a night on their exercise wheels!

Defensive Balling

If startled by a sudden sound or movement, a hedgehog will pull their visor quills down and curl into a tight ball. Never try to force a hedgehog to uncurl; wait for them to feel safe.

Scent Recognition

Hedgehogs recognize their owners by smell. Placing a worn t-shirt in their cage can help a new hedgehog get used to your specific scent and feel more comfortable around you.
