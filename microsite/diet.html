What Do Hedgehogs Eat?

Hedgehogs are primarily insectivores. In the wild they spend most of the night foraging for insects, worms, beetles, larvae, and other small invertebrates. This high-protein diet supports their metabolism, muscle development, and natural energy levels.

Pet hedgehogs require a balanced diet that mimics this natural feeding pattern. Most owners use high-quality cat food as the main base because it contains the appropriate protein levels hedgehogs need. This base diet should then be supplemented with insects, small portions of fruits and vegetables, and occasional treats.

Maintaining a proper diet is extremely important for pet hedgehogs because they are prone to obesity when overfed or given too many fatty treats. A good diet focuses on lean protein, fiber, and controlled portion sizes.

Diet Guidelines

  • Main Diet: High-protein dry cat food
  • Protein Target: 28–35%
  • Fat Target: 10–15%
  • Activity: Mostly nocturnal feeding
  • Fresh Water: Always available

Core Diet: High-Quality Cat Food

Most hedgehog owners rely on premium dry cat food as the staple of their hedgehog’s diet. Cat food is widely available and provides the protein hedgehogs need when insects are not the primary food source.

When selecting cat food, it is important to choose brands with high protein content, moderate fat levels, and real meat listed as the first ingredient. Avoid foods with excessive fillers such as corn or artificial additives.

  • Look for protein levels between 28% and 35%
  • Fat should remain between 10% and 15%
  • Choose smaller kibble sizes when possible
  • Avoid foods with excessive sugars or fillers

Insects and Natural Protein Sources

Insects are a natural and enriching part of a hedgehog’s diet. They mimic what hedgehogs eat in the wild and provide mental stimulation when used as treats or enrichment feeding.

  • Mealworms
  • Crickets
  • Waxworms (occasionally)
  • Dubia roaches
  • Silkworms

While insects are beneficial, they should be fed in moderation because many contain high fat levels. Mealworms and waxworms especially should be treated as occasional treats rather than daily food sources.

Safe Fruits and Vegetables

Fruits and vegetables can provide fiber, vitamins, and variety in a hedgehog’s diet. However, they should only make up a small portion of their food intake. Always introduce new foods slowly to avoid digestive issues.

  • Apples (without seeds)
  • Blueberries
  • Bananas (small portions)
  • Carrots
  • Peas
  • Cooked sweet potato
  • Green beans

Foods to Avoid

Some foods are unsafe or toxic to hedgehogs and should never be included in their diet. These foods can cause digestive issues, toxicity, or long-term health problems.

  • Dairy products
  • Chocolate
  • Grapes and raisins
  • Onions and garlic
  • Raw meat
  • Highly processed foods
  • Salty or sugary snacks

Feeding Schedule

Because hedgehogs are nocturnal animals, they should typically be fed during the evening when they are most active.

  • Evening: Main portion of kibble
  • Occasional insects for enrichment
  • Small fruit or vegetable portions 1–2 times per week
  • Fresh water available at all times

Adult hedgehogs usually eat between one and two tablespoons of food each day, but this may vary depending on their size, activity level, and metabolism.
