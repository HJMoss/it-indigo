Diet

What Do Hedgehogs Eat?

Hedgehogs are primarily insectivores. In the wild they spend most of the night foraging for insects, worms, beetles, larvae, and other small invertebrates.

Pet hedgehogs require a balanced diet that mimics this natural feeding pattern. Most owners use high-quality cat food as the main base supplemented with insects and veggies.

Maintaining a proper diet is extremely important because hedgehogs are prone to obesity when overfed.

Diet Guidelines

  • Main Diet: High-protein dry cat food
  • Protein Target: 28–35%
  • Fat Target: 10–15%
  • Activity: Mostly nocturnal feeding
  • Fresh Water: Always available

Core Diet: Cat Food

Most owners rely on premium dry cat food as a staple. Choose brands with real meat as the first ingredient.

  • Protein: 28% – 35%
  • Fat: 10% – 15%

Safe Insects

Insects mimic what hedgehogs eat in the wild and provide vital mental stimulation.

  • Mealworms & Crickets
  • Dubia roaches

Fruits & Veggies

These provide fiber and vitamins, but should only be a small portion of the diet.

  • Apples (no seeds)
  • Blueberries

Toxic Foods

  • Chocolate
  • Grapes and raisins
  • Onions and garlic

Health Risks

  • Dairy products
  • Raw meat
  • Salty or sugary snacks
