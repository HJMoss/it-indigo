What is a Hedgehog?

Hedgehogs are small mammals characterized by their coat of stiff, sharp spines. When threatened, they can roll into a tight ball, protecting their soft underbelly. Despite their prickly exterior, they are known for their inquisitive nature and distinctive "snuffling" sounds as they forage.

Quick Facts

  • Lifespan: 4-6 years
  • Diet: Insectivores
  • Activity: Nocturnal
  • Temp: 72°F - 80°F
  • Spines: 5,000 - 7,000
  • Speed: 6 ft/sec
  • Classification: Erinaceinae
  • Ancestry: Not related to Porcupines
  • Genetics: Not related to Tenrecs
  • Heart Rate: 190 bpm
  • Hibernation: Facultative
  • Self-Anointing: Frothing behavior
  • Litter Size: 3-4 Hoglets
  • Social: Solitary
  • Gestation: 32-48 days
  • Weight: 0.5 - 1.5 lbs
