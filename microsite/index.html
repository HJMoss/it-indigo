What Is a Hedgehog?

Hedgehogs are small, spiny mammals known for their unique appearance and defensive behavior. When threatened, they roll into a tight ball, causing their sharp spines to point outward to protect themselves from predators.

Basic Facts

  • Hedgehogs are nocturnal animals.
  • They are found in Europe, Africa, and Asia.
  • Their bodies are covered in thousands of protective spines.
  • They rely heavily on smell and hearing.
  • Average lifespan: 3–5 years in the wild.
